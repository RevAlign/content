## Tuesday: Your Funnel is Missing Half

**Graphic:** `01_bowtie_funnel.png`
**Pillar:** RevOps for Early-Stage

Most SaaS funnels end at Closed Won.

That's exactly where 50%+ of your revenue actually happens.

The traditional funnel treats the sale as the finish line. But for recurring revenue businesses, the real work starts after someone signs.

Onboarding. Adoption. Expansion. Renewal.

If your lifecycle stages stop at "Customer," you have zero visibility into:

- Which accounts are stuck in onboarding
- Who's actually using the product
- Where expansion opportunities exist
- Which renewals are at risk

The Bowtie model fixes this by treating Closed Won as the midpoint, not the endpoint.

Left side: Awareness → Education → Selection → Commitment
Right side: Onboarding → Impact → Expansion

Same CRM. Different architecture. Complete visibility.

## Wednesday: The 4 Revenue Levers

**Graphic:** `02_four_levers.png`
**Pillar:** GTM Infrastructure

Not all revenue improvements are created equal.

Volume is additive. More leads = proportionally more pipeline. Linear.

Conversion is multiplicative. A 5% lift across 4 stages compounds to 22% improvement.

Deal size is exponential. Same effort, bigger outcomes. Works best late-funnel.

Retention is compound. The highest leverage lever. NRR is your growth engine.

Most teams obsess over Volume because it's the easiest to measure.

The best teams optimize Retention first because a 5% improvement in retention can mean 25%+ profit increase.

Before you hire another SDR, ask: What's our NRR? What's our time-to-value? What's our expansion rate?

Volume matters. But it's not the only lever.

Which lever is your team pulling right now?

## Thursday: The Lifecycle Stage Mistake

**Graphic:** `03_lifecycle_mistake.png`
**Pillar:** HubSpot Truths

HubSpot's default lifecycle stages:
Subscriber → Lead → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Customer

That's where it stops.

But for SaaS, "Customer" isn't one stage. It's at least 5:

- Onboarding (actively implementing)
- Active (achieving regular usage)
- Impact Achieved (measurable outcomes)
- Renewal Ready (within 90 days)
- Growth Account (expanded/multi-product)

Without these, your CS team is flying blind. You can't report on:

- Onboarding completion rates
- Time-to-value by segment
- Expansion pipeline
- At-risk renewals

The fix takes 5 minutes:
Settings → Data Management → Objects → Lifecycle Stage → Add custom stages

One configuration change. Complete post-sale visibility.

What stages do you have after "Customer"?

## Friday: The Hidden Bottleneck

**Graphic:** `04_time_metrics.png`
**Pillar:** Revenue Operations

You track how many leads you generate.

Do you track how long each stage takes?

Time metrics reveal bottlenecks that volume and conversion hide:

Δt1: Sessions → Lead (engagement lag)
Δt2: Lead → MQL (nurture purgatory)
Δt3: MQL → SQL (sales response time)
Δt4: Opportunity → Close (the one everyone tracks)
Δt5: Close → First Value (the one nobody tracks)
Δt7: Close → Upsell (expansion velocity)

Revenue leaks aren't always about volume or conversion.

Sometimes the problem is lag.

A company with 20% win rate and 45-day cycle beats a company with 25% win rate and 90-day cycle.

Every day a deal sits is a day your competitor could close it.

What's your time-to-value for new customers?
